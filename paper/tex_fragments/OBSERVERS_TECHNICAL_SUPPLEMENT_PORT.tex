\subsection{Cosmological Principle}

\paragraph{\textbf{Lemma 4.1 (MaxEnt symmetry inheritance).}}
Let \(G\) act on the screen by automorphisms \(\alpha_g\). If the constraint set is
\(G\)-invariant and von Neumann entropy is \(G\)-invariant, then the MaxEnt optimizer \(\omega\)
can be taken \(G\)-invariant. If the optimizer is unique, it is automatically \(G\)-invariant.

\paragraph{\textbf{Theorem 4.2 (Isotropy from SO(3)-invariant constraints).}}
Under SO(3)-invariant constraints and MaxEnt uniqueness, the reference state satisfies
\[
\omega\circ\alpha_g=\omega
\qquad
\forall g\in \mathrm{SO}(3).
\]
The corresponding stress tensor has perfect-fluid form:
\[
\langle T_{ab}\rangle=\rho\,u_au_b+p(g_{ab}+u_au_b).
\]

\paragraph{\textbf{Theorem 4.3 (Schur-type homogeneity).}}
Let \((\Sigma,h_{ij})\) be a three-dimensional Riemannian manifold. If at every point
\(p\in\Sigma\), the curvature tensor is SO(3)-invariant, then
\[
R_{ijkl}(p)=K(p)(h_{ik}h_{jl}-h_{il}h_{jk}),
\]
and the second Bianchi identity forces \(\nabla_m K=0\), so \(K\) is constant.

\paragraph{\textbf{Corollary 4.4.}}
If OPH supplies isotropy for all observers, spatial geometry is a constant-curvature space form:
\(S^3\), \(\mathbb R^3\), or \(H^3\).

\paragraph{\textbf{Theorem 4.5 (FLRW emergence).}}
With the semiclassical Einstein equation from recoverable generalized entropy / entanglement
equilibrium, a perfect-fluid stress tensor from SO(3)-invariant MaxEnt, and positive \(\Lambda\)
from finite screen capacity, the emergent geometry is FLRW:

\[
H^2+\frac{k}{a^2}=\frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho+\frac{\Lambda}{3},
\qquad
\dot H-\frac{k}{a^2}=-4\pi G(\rho+p).
