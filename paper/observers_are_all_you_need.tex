\begin{abstract}
Observer-Patch Holography is a reverse-engineering program. Start with a finite holographic screen. Close a self-reference between the outer pixel of the simulating screen and the inner fine-structure constant seen by observers inside the simulated universe. That fixed point gives the fine-structure constant and the pixel ratio \(P\). Feed that scale into the overlap-consistency machinery and the theory yields the Lorentz/Einstein branch, the realized Standard Model quotient with exact hypercharge and counting data, the main particle package, and the worldsheet/string lane. This paper puts the full chain on one surface and states the remaining open derivation fronts, with charged-lepton absolute masses and hadron closure unresolved.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

This synthesis paper reads the OPH suite on one unified synthesis surface. It brings
together the recovered core from Ref.~\cite{ophcompact}, the finite patch-net consensus spine from
Ref.~\cite{ophconsensus}, the screen-microphysics engineering lane from Ref.~\cite{ophmicrophysics},
and the downstream particle-spectrum lane from Ref.~\cite{ophparticles}. The guiding
question is whether overlap consistency for local observer descriptions on a finite-capacity screen
is strong enough to recover the structural features of low-energy physics, and if so, how much of
that recovery is theorem-level and how much depends on explicit scaling-limit,
categorical, or continuation premises.

On this synthesis surface, recovered-core theorem status and claim tier inherit from the compact recovered-core ledger in Ref.~\cite{ophcompact}, while lane-specific claim tier remains with the corresponding companion papers, including Refs.~\cite{ophparticles,ophconsensus,ophmicrophysics}; the present paper organizes those outputs across lanes but does not upgrade them by restating them.

The answer is sharply tiered. Fixed-cutoff collar structure, higher-gauge defect transport,
the consensus/fixed-point formulation together with observable-level quotient confluence on the
declared physical algebras, the
record-algebra / Born-L\"uders package with its quantitative stability surface, and the
checkpoint/restoration package, including checkpoint data, restoration maps, future-law stability under restoration error, and an operational observer-identity criterion imported from Ref.~\cite{ophmicrophysics}, are theorem-bearing on their stated finite
surfaces. The Lorentz/null-modular/Einstein chain remains a conditional scaling-limit branch,
starting from finite type-I regulators but targeting an extracted scaling-limit observer algebra
that may leave that class, and compact gauge reconstruction remains a conditional categorical branch. The particle lane is the most tiered part of
the suite: the structural carrier skeleton and realized Standard Model branch are fixed, the
electroweak stage remains a forward-emitted non-core Phase-II quantitative-closure branch whose internal
transmutation data are fixed from \(P\), with target-free public \(W/Z\) rows and a compare-only
frozen validation pair, the Higgs/top stage is an exact source-only D11 split theorem on the declared D10/D11 running, matching, and threshold surface with a compare-only exact inverse slice beneath it, the neutrino lane
closes on its weighted-cycle theorem branch with diagnostic sidecars beneath it, the charged-lepton lane is fixed
only up to exact centered readback plus a closed common-shift no-go, the quark lane closes on the
public physical quark frame class selected by \(P\) without claiming a global classification of all
quark frame classes, and the hadron lane remains unfinished.

The paper is organized by lane. Section~2 gives the synthesis-level map. Section~3 carries the
recovered-core trunk: axioms, premise ledger, collar tools, gluing, the Lorentz/gravity branch,
and the compact-gauge/Standard-Model branch. Section~4 records the consensus lane. Section~5
summarizes the particle lane. Section~6 records the screen-microphysics and observer lane.
Section~7 records the conditional worldsheet lane. Section~8 gives the cross-lane theorem
boundary and open directions. The appendices collect supplementary architecture and synthesis-level
material that is useful in this paper but not part of the recovered core itself.

\section{Overview of Results}

The OPH suite supports the following lane-level split.

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{At fixed cutoff, local structure and repair are sharp.} Technically, the
collar theorem package is explicit and finite-range, the genuinely noncentral higher-gauge branch
is closed at fixed cutoff by the crossed-module defect package, and the consensus lane gives a
unique schedule-independent quotient normal form together with schedule-independent physical
observable values on the declared fixed-cutoff physical algebras once the declared
recovery-derived repair moves are committed by the touched-overlap local-fit contract that yields
strict decrease of the inconsistency potential \(\Phi\), disjoint accepted repairs commute by
support locality, the fixed-cutoff center-sector / higher-gauge gluing package makes overlapping
repairs parenthesization-independent on the physical quotient, and repair completeness holds on the
stated finite patch-net branch. The remaining open item on that genuinely noncentral lane is the
refinement-limit transportable-sector lift used by the later compact-gauge branch. Microscopic
representative lifts may still differ by gauge or sector relabelings inside one declared
quotient-local glued state without changing the resulting observable values on those algebras.
\item \textbf{Relativity appears as a controlled continuum branch.}
Technically, the Lorentz/null-modular/Einstein chain is a refinement/scaling-limit branch.
Its D3 side depends on the \textbf{T2} scaling-limit scope clause together with the theorem-local
realized transported geometric cap-local system whose carried-collar schedule sits above the
transported fixed-local-collar Markov/faithfulness datum, and then ordered cut-pair rigidity on
the extracted prime geometric subnet; on that branch the finite cap regulators are type-I, but the
scaling-limit observer algebra may be non-type-I, so the theorem is automorphism-level there and
\(K_C=2\pi B_C\) is only the special type-I limit form; stronger BW / canonical-cap phrasing
remains theorem-external unless the extraction-and-rigidity package is supplied. Its D4 side still uses the theorem-local null-cut center
transfer, the extra inherited left/right strip-split condition needed for the spatial-collar-type
tensor decomposition, the exact-or-controlled Markov hypotheses on one fixed inherited strip
model, and the geometric scaling action on the null half-line blow-up net that derives the
half-sided modular pair, while the same-half-line local modular-Hamiltonian input and the
separate bounded-interval projective branch enter only later; on that D4 surface the
exact-or-controlled strip additivity, the endpoint-Lipschitz renormalized half-line family, the
weak tail generator, the derived half-sided modular inclusion, and the positive null-translation
generator on its Stone domain with the affine half-line modular relation are internal to the
bridge itself; and its D5 side adds fixed-cap stationarity,
the locally Lorentzian \(d=4\) scaling regime, and the tensor-upgrade side conditions.
\item \textbf{The Standard Model gauge skeleton appears as a constrained consistency output.}
Technically, compact gauge reconstruction and the realized
\(\SU(3)\times\SU(2)\times\U(1)/\mathbb Z_6\) branch follow under the stated transportability and
categorical premises, with the exact hypercharge lattice and the realized counting chain \(N_g=3\) then \(N_c=3\).
\item \textbf{The particle lane has real outputs and a nonuniform closure profile.}
Technically, the structural carriers are exact, the electroweak stage remains on a non-core
Phase-II quantitative-closure branch with target-free public \(W/Z\) rows, a Ward-projected
electromagnetic transport family whose low-energy endpoint is emitted by the same forward
closure program, and a compare-only frozen validation pair, its internal
transmutation data are fixed forward from \(P\) rather than read back from measured couplings, the
Higgs/top stage closes on a source-only split theorem surface, the charged-lepton lane carries
exact centered readback plus a closed common-shift no-go, the neutrino lane closes on its
weighted-cycle theorem branch with diagnostic sidecars below it, the quark lane closes on the
public physical quark frame class selected by \(P\) without claiming a global classification of
all quark frame classes, and the hadron lane remains an execution-contract surface rather than a
mass-derivation theorem lane; at the local numerical level the same declared input \(P\) organizes
the bosonic mass trunk and the gravity-side shared edge-entropy identity beneath the local \(G\)
readout.
\item \textbf{The screen-microphysics lane is explicit and concrete.} Technically, it
contains an explicit regulated reference architecture, sharpened patch-net and edge-sector
branches, a closed fixed-cutoff record-algebra / Born-L\"uders package with quantitative stability
bounds, and a closed checkpoint/restoration package.
\item \textbf{The UV picture is sharper at fixed cutoff than in the continuum.} Technically, OPH
fixes the physical UV branch only modulo implementation hiding and inert ancillary refinement; the
UV burden is the realized transported geometric cap-local system with its derived carried-collar
schedule above the transported fixed-local-collar Markov/faithfulness datum, then ordered
cut-pair rigidity on the emitted scaling-limit geometric cap pair, and separately the
refinement-limit transportable-sector lift.
\item \textbf{The consensus lane is exact but not overpromoted.} Technically, on each fixed finite
patch net the normal form is a finite-state decidable object, approximate stability is only
carried by the local splice and record error controls, and no universality theorem for a uniform
family of growing patch nets and repair laws is part of the five-paper stack.
\item \textbf{The string/worldsheet lane remains conditional.} Technically, it is carried only as
a continuation of the OPH heat-kernel edge-sector theorem, not as part of the recovered core.
\end{enumerate}
