\begin{abstract}
Observer-Patch Holography (OPH) asks whether familiar physics can be rebuilt from a very small
starting point: no observer sees the whole world at once, each observer has access only to local
data, and neighboring descriptions must agree where they overlap. In that picture, spacetime,
gauge structure, particles, and classical records are not put in by hand at the start. They are
features of whatever survives global consistency among many partial viewpoints on a finite-capacity
screen.

This paper is the current synthesis of that program. It brings together the fixed-cutoff collar
theorems, the consensus/fixed-point formulation of overlap repair, the current route to Lorentzian
geometry and Einstein dynamics, the reconstruction route to compact gauge structure and the
realized Standard Model quotient
\(\SU(3)\times\SU(2)\times\U(1)/\mathbb Z_6\), the present particle-spectrum branch, and the
fixed-cutoff measurement and observer package. Some parts of that story are already theorem-level,
especially at fixed cutoff; others remain conditional on explicit scaling-limit or categorical
premises. The aim of the synthesis paper is to show, in one place, what OPH is trying to do, what
it currently achieves, and where the real open problems still are. On the particle side, the
electroweak calibration chain is now closed on its active Phase II surface: a target-free
source-only theorem emits one coherent electroweak quintet \((W,Z,\alpha_{\mathrm{em}}^{-1},
\sin^2\theta_W,v)\); the charged-lepton lane fixes the centered hierarchy but not yet the
absolute normalization; the neutrino continuation machinery closes numerically on a branch that is
still quantitatively wrong; and the wider quark and hadron lanes remain at later status tiers.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

This paper presents the current OPH program on a single theorem and status surface. The guiding
question is whether overlap consistency for local observer descriptions on a finite-capacity
screen is strong enough to recover the structural features of low-energy physics. In the present
formulation the answer is tiered. Fixed-cutoff collar structure, higher-gauge defect transport,
and the consensus/fixed-point formulation are theorem-bearing. The Lorentz/null-modular/Einstein
chain and compact gauge reconstruction require explicit scaling-limit or categorical premises. The
particle-spectrum and worldsheet branches contain additional calibration and continuation layers
whose status is stated separately. The strongest current particle-side result is that the
electroweak calibration lane is closed on a target-free source-only theorem surface, while the
later matter-family lanes remain explicitly split by theorem, continuation, branch-repair, and
execution-bound status.

The paper is organized accordingly. Section~2 records the synthesis-level outputs. The central
trunk then presents the OPH axioms, information-theoretic tools, collar analysis, gravity branch,
and gauge branch. The later synthesis sections add the consensus formulation, the
particle-spectrum branch, the fixed-cutoff measurement and observer package, and the conditional
worldsheet continuation. The appendices collect the candidate microphysics architecture together
with theorem material that appears only in the synthesis paper.

\section{Overview of Results}

The OPH suite currently supports the following synthesis-level claim split.

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{At fixed cutoff, neighboring descriptions can be stitched together in a precise and
interacting way.} Technically, the collar theorem package is explicit, finite-range, and now
includes the higher-gauge closure of the genuinely noncentral topological branch.
\item \textbf{Relativity appears as a controlled continuum branch rather than an extra axiom.}
Technically, the Lorentz/null-modular/Einstein chain is a refinement/scaling-limit branch with
explicit canonical technical premises.
\item \textbf{The Standard Model gauge skeleton appears as a constrained consistency output.}
Technically, compact gauge reconstruction and the realized
\(\SU(3)\times\SU(2)\times\U(1)/\mathbb Z_6\) branch follow under the stated transportability and
categorical premises.
\item \textbf{The particle story is no longer outside the synthesis paper.} Technically, the
particle-spectrum branch is included here with an explicit split between structural rows, a closed
electroweak calibration theorem surface, later quantitative rows, open continuation rows, and
hadron rows that remain execution-bound rather than theorem-emitted.
\item \textbf{Measurement and observer persistence have a fixed-cutoff theorem surface.}
Technically, the Born-Luders record package and the observer checkpoint/restoration package are
part of the main synthesis.
\item \textbf{The string/worldsheet branch remains interesting but clearly conditional.}
Technically, it is presented as a continuation of the OPH heat-kernel edge-sector theorem rather
than as part of the recovered core.
\end{enumerate}
